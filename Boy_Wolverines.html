Boys' Athletic Coordinator:
Coach Littleton

William Littleton was a multi-event track athlete, excelling specifically in the high jump event where he was a 2-time high school state champion (2003-'04), with a personal best jump of 6'10." His successes in high school earned him an athletic scholarship to compete at Angelo State University (D-II) in San Angelo, TX. While at Angelo State University (2004-'05), he won two Division II titles. After experiencing success at the Division 2 level, he transferred to UT-Pan American University (Division 1) to challenge himself at the highest level of collegiate competition. While at UT-Pan American (2005-'08), he rose to fame by breaking three school records and winning three meets in six days. His personal best jump in college was 7' 5 3/4." He is a 4-time D-1 All-American and 7-time All-American. After college, he turned pro and set his sights on the 2008 Olympics and finished 10th in the Olympic Trials and finished 5th at the USA Indoor Championships in 2010. He is now the athletic coordinator and head football coach at PSJA Memorial ECHS where he has reignited the flame not just with the football program, but with the school spirit of the fans as well.
