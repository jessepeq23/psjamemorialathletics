Outcomes of Joining Track & Field

Stronger

Who doesn’t want to be stronger? Be ready to become much stronger as you push past your limits.

Faster

Being faster is something us as humans have always strived toward. As a famous quote says, “slow lions don’t eat”.

Endurance

Building endurance does not mean running forever. The ability to use your heart and lungs until the job is done is real endurance.

Family

You may have heard the saying, “there is no I in team”, but here at PSJA Memorial we take that to heart. By joining, be ready to meet your new brothers and sisters.

A FEW WORDS

“Future Wolverines should know that there is a big difference between sports in middle school and high school. They need to know their reason for joining, so that reason can carry them for 4 years and drive them to want to improve every day. I want my athletes to graduate with a sense of accomplishment, but also with a hunger to be better than they were in high school. I want them to be prepared to set real-life goals and know that failure is not an option in the future. It’s like my grandfather always told me, ‘if it’s been done before you can do it too’”.

Coach Cazares
Boys Track Head Coach

“I was not ready for the adversity I was going to face, but throughout the years it made me physically and mentally stronger. My coaches were a big help in my athletic career, they helped me out and stuck by me when I felt like giving up. The consistency of me showing up showed I was not going to let my challenges get the best of me. I would tell the future generation looking to join track how this sport is tough; not only physically but mentally. You must prepare for the harsh practices and have the courage to keep goin when you can’t feel your body anymore. That strength doesn’t come from strength training, it comes from overcoming the things you once thought were impossible.”

Aliyah Ortiz
Varsity Athlete Class of 2023
